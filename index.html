about me

i am a graphics journalist working as a data visualization and information graphics designer at pew research center in washington, d.c.

before that, i interned on npr's graphics and digital news desks, where i analyzed and visualized data and pitched and reported stories across beats. i previously worked with chalkbeat and usa today's data teams. at indiana university, i worked as an enterprise editor and reporter on the award-winning staff at the indiana daily student.

i'm more interested in using data to tell compelling, graphics-driven stories than in any given beat. because of that, i've covered a wide array of topics and institutions, including hospital price negotiations, access to books in prison, and child labor.

in my current role, i work closely with researchers and web developers to make important findings shine on the web. recent examples include designing a photo illustration for a groundbreaking study on america's news influencers and illustrating sprites to animate an interactive essay about teen life today.

i'm also a developer on the center's R graphics package, which streamlines chartmaking in R and customizes ggplot2 to our style guide. if you're interested in doing something similar at your org, here's the github repo from a hands-on class i taught at nicar 2025.
